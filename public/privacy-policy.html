Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

What we collect

When you use VectraCore, we collect:

  • The email address you provide to unlock beta access, and confirmation that you verified it via a one-time code.
  • Chart images you upload for analysis.
  • The questions you ask and the analysis history tied to your account.
  • Feedback messages you submit through our contact or feedback forms.

How we use it

Your email and analysis history are used to give you a continuous workspace — so past chart analyses and conversations are available when you come back. We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

Marketing emails

By checking "I agree to all policies" when you sign up, you agree that VectraCore may send you emails about product updates, new features, discounts, and other promotional offers, in addition to the verification and account emails needed to run the service.

You can opt out of marketing emails at any time by using the unsubscribe link included in those emails, or by contacting us through our Contact Us page and asking to be removed. Opting out of marketing emails does not affect account, security, or verification emails, which we may still need to send you to operate the service.

Third-party processing

To generate analysis, chart images and questions are sent to Google's Gemini AI models for processing. Market news headlines used in answers are sourced from Finnhub. Neither provider is used to build advertising profiles on you — they process your request and return a result.

Local storage

Your email is stored in your browser's local storage so you don't have to re-enter it each visit. Clearing your browser data will remove it.

Data retention

Analysis history is retained so you can review past sessions. You can request deletion of your data at any time by contacting us.

Contact

Questions about this policy or your data can be sent through our Contact Us page.

VectraCore is currently in free public beta. This policy will be updated as the product evolves. This page is provided as a general policy template and has not been reviewed by a lawyer — consider a legal review before relying on it for a commercial launch.
